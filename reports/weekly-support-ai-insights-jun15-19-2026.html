Top 3 UI issues this week

1

Deposits & payouts — "paid in DoorLoop, but not in my bank"

Payment status, payout timing, and bank reconciliation
93 escalations · 15% of all
CX 64.1%

The dominant pattern: a payment shows as paid / deposited inside DoorLoop, but the money isn't in the customer's bank — or the amounts don't match. Users can't reconcile what the app shows against their bank statement, and Fin can't resolve a real funds-movement discrepancy, so it escalates.

Where it breaks (UI / product)

  • Payout visibility: "Paid" status with no money received, and no in-app explanation of holds or timing.
  • Bank reconciliation: deposits show as uncleared but don't appear in bank transactions; bundled deposits can't be matched; can't add/delete a transaction.
  • Security deposit recording: two-step charge-and-payment is confusing; withholding a deposit shows a negative / unexpected refund.

In customers' words

  • "Why was only $885 transferred when my tenant paid $1,250?" (CX 1)
  • "Where is the money that shows as deposited but is not in my bank account?" (CX 3)
  • "It shows paid on June 12th, but the money hasn't appeared after 5 days." (CX 2)
  • "Why do deposits show as uncleared on reconciliation but don't appear under bank transactions?" (CX 4)
  • "Why does the deposit tab show a negative number when I withhold the deposit?" (CX 5)
2

Leases — renewals, e-signature, and moving balances

Lease lifecycle workflows
84 escalations · 13% of all
CX 65.5%

Escalations cluster in three lease workflows where the UI blocks users mid-task: renewals, the e-signature / document flow, and moving tenants or balances between leases. These are multi-step flows where a single error or missing button stops the user cold.

Where it breaks (UI / product)

  • Renewals: "dates conflict" errors, renewal offers auto-sent, renewed/month-to-month leases not appearing in the Renewals queue, double first-month charges.
  • E-signature / docs: can't upload an existing signed lease for signature, missing signature field, no Save button on the template editor.
  • Moving tenants / balances: can't transfer a balance or payment from a new lease back to the original; "NEXT" button disabled when ending a lease.

In customers' words

  • "Why is there no save button when I finish creating a lease template?" (CX 2)
  • "Why can I only create a new lease and not upload an existing signed lease for signature?" (CX 2)
  • "Why can't the tenant see the signature field on the renewal lease?" (CX 4)
  • "How do I resolve the 'dates conflict' error when renewing my lease?" (CX 3)
  • "How can I transfer the balance from the new lease back to the old lease?" (CX 5)
3

Merchant accounts — Stripe verification & setup

Getting set up and staying verified to receive payouts
40 escalations · 6% of all
CX 53.8%

Two sub-issues make up nearly all of this topic: Stripe account access & verification (18) and merchant account setup & application (17). Users get blocked or stuck onboarding to payments — something Fin can't push through, so it escalates.

Where it breaks (UI / product)

  • Verification loop: repeated "verification incomplete" emails on accounts that already show active/verified; re-verification triggered by an address mismatch.
  • Setup friction: users say the merchant application "is not straightforward"; verification codes sent to old email/phone; tenants blocked after too many attempts.
  • Silent failures: card payments show "in progress" to the tenant but leave no record in DoorLoop or Stripe.

In customers' words

  • "Why am I still getting 'verification incomplete' emails when my accounts are active and verified?" (CX 5)
  • "Why am I being asked to re-verify all my merchant accounts due to an address issue?" (CX 2)
  • "How can I verify my bank account if Stripe sends codes to my old, deactivated email and phone?" (CX 3)
  • "Card payments show 'in progress' but leave no record — silent failures." (CX 4)
  • "Why can't I complete Stripe verification due to the questions asked about my account?" (CX 3)

Cross-cutting root cause

⚠ The Stripe payment-processing migration is feeding both #1 and #3

In multiple escalations, the support team's own explanation points to the in-progress migration to a new Stripe payment structure that adds a high-risk holding period. During the hold, DoorLoop displays the full pre-hold payout amount while the bank receives less — so the app's numbers don't match the bank, generating "missing funds," "$1 off," and reconciliation escalations. Reporting also lags. This single backend change is a meaningful driver of Deposits and Merchant accounts volume — worth aligning Product, Payments, and CX on messaging and in-app transparency while the migration completes.
